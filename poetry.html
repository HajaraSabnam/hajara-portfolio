And these are a few of my poems

White


I wonder,
What do these clouds hide?
The gloom it’s about to glaze me with
Or the glory to conceal the dark?
Like it does
With itself
One can never know, I suppose.
And perhaps, that’s the reason
It’s too appealing to the eyes
Yet so unreal to think about.


Spirit


It feels like just yesterday
I came into this world in spring
But look, it’s already autumn.
Along with the petals of soft flowers,
I wished to fly away.
But if the flowers perished
Where is the beauty of the plant, I thought.
And so, I stayed.
For a pitied reason
Only to find that
The plant bore the flowers
And hence there is so much green
Than the glowing hue
To gracefully endow me
With a steady and placid life
Of opportunities to blossom
And not just fugitive flowers
That end up faded and forgotten.


Time, A Saviour


Lamps of colors blinds the shades of those eyes
A beautiful little face
Sometimes, she smiles and several times, she frowns
Isn’t that how dubious life is?
Times filled with happiness and those with pain
Before I am done with one, here it is, another one.
Before I realize that my hair’s grown a few inches,
I feel the wrinkles on my face as I laugh
I sometimes look back at that beautiful face
That has withered with time
Such were the shades, dark and bright
Moments I am caged up
Now that I think about it,
Oh, how lucky am I
That you never let go of my hand
That you never let me stop
That you always made me run with you
A savior, I never realized I had
Before I put down this pen that I am holding
A thousand frowns have let go of me
A few smiles have come looking for me
Thanks to you, I am still running with you
Time changes everything
And yet life goes on, doesn’t it?

Melodies of the Wind


As I put my ear close to the windowpane
I feel the breeze with a heavy heart
Warm and cold is the usual rhythm
But this time, it’s different
This wind holds onto stories of many
Among troubles, one speaks happiness
There’s one with tears too
Several throughout the night
To keep me well occupied
That night filled my ears with words
Some I never want to utter
Some I wish I had heard earlier
In the meantime, this heart forgot
What it had been holding the night before
Give some thought to it
Time passes and so does life, doesn’t it?
When there is so much weight in their hearts
What am I holding onto?
This little soul is blessed, I felt
They would feel the same, I thought
If they had heard each other
In a life filled with troubles
There is an ease in sharing
In a world filled with such souls
There are stories waiting to be shared
And this is the music
The wind plays every night
This time, listen closely
You will hear one too.


Words


To be oneself, is to live at ease
To feel happy, embracing life like an evening breeze.
In every moment, shall find peace
To feel content, with one’s own feats.

To laugh along, at life's subtle play
Forgetting little errors, ones of life's display.
In simple joys, to find one’s way
Is somehow, only to exist in words, like these convey.


Blue Hour


When the blue hour hits
Midnight, when it's past 12
Even the daring waves
Look calm and quiet
Something about the blues of the nights
Watching the world rush
When my heart takes a little rest
Rich of calmness in a hush
Loaded with nostalgic beauty
How is it, you calm me so much? I asked
For that's when you see the world
With no expectations and no thoughts
Simply, when your world is quiet, it said.


Silenced


In the dead of the night, when the world fell quiet
A voice spoke out of an uncertain conviction.
Yearning for a glint, the moonlight embraced her little face
A face that has withered over time
A heart that had battled itself
A mind that forced her to keep fighting
She had carried a lot, the miserable woman.
But who bothered to ask her, she thought
As she reminisced her life, she let out a laugh
Soaked in pain, her lips hardly curled
Just smile, be elegant.
You are a girl, they said.
As a sharp pain caught her throat
She screamed with tears.
Be polite, be quiet.
You are a girl, they said.
As she prepared to speak
Listen to him.
Again, you are the girl, they said.
And today, as the veil covered her pale face
She felt her dreams shatter into pieces
Amidst the voices of the unspoken,
Her voice stayed mute.
Amidst the rules of the unbothered,
Her thoughts stayed with her.
Amidst their system of life
She became a mere puppet.
In their world, filled with lights
She was devoid of one.
And that is how it was supposed to be, they said.
But who are they, she thought as she closed her eyes.
With filtered emotions, she lifted up her face
Hoping that her voice would echo in their unfiltered minds,
She opened her eyes.
A gentle breeze touched her slow
In the soft embrace of the light, she felt a power
Of a world that had truly fell silent.
And there she disappeared into the moonlight
Nowhere to be found again.

Pace


Time ticks quick
But here I stand still
Amidst the buzz of a world
That never seems to settle
To step ahead
Means to break barriers
To switch circumstances
Means to fear and quit
Have I lost my time already?
But allow me to ask
Who times our efforts?
A mind racing a heart isn’t life.
For, a pace that’s too fast
Could never lead to a stable end
And hence there is no fault
In a pace
That’s steady and poise.

~Hajara Sabnam
